Mechanical Design Project

This Mechanical Design Report was written as the semester project of the EML3005 (Mechanical Design One) class here at the University of Florida. This project based on my 2004 Ninja 250 motorcycle. It consisted of removing and gathering data off of the motorcycle, learning latex for the formatting, processing data in an excel spreadsheet, modeling various parts of the motorcycle in solidworks, and running a static solidworks von mises' stress simulation on said parts.

Getting Started/Gather Data

Measurements of the various parts needed to be taken in order to ensure accurate results. A caliper was used to further reduce the uncertainty of any measurements. Measurements of the brake lever, rear wheel swingarm, rear wheel sprocket, and front brake caliper. The swingarm, cash chain tensioner push rod, and front/rear brake pads were removed from the motorcycle to take the measurements. The rest of the measurements were taken while the part was on the motorcycle. These measurements were compiled onto an excel spreadsheet which can be downloaded below:
Excel Spreadsheet: Databook.xlsx

Learning Latex

The project was done in Latex using the Miktex console and the Texmaker GUI as recommeded by my controls professor. The kaobook template was used as the backbone for the formatting struture of the report.

Solidworks Modeling

With the data in the excel spreadsheet, all the parts were modeled into solidworks. Due to the swingarm's complex geometry it was removed from the motorcycle to ensure an accurate model. The various solidworks files can be downloaded below:
